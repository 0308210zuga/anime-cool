Anime, like other video productions, is created with a particular distribution in mind[57][58]. These methods include showing on television, in cinemas, selling on video media, distribution via the Internet.

A movie is an anime intended to be shown in a cinema. As a rule, these are full-length (from 60 minutes) films, which are distinguished by a higher quality of animation compared to other formats and, accordingly, higher production costs.

ONA (Original Network Animation) "animation created for the Internet" - an anime specially created for distribution on the Internet. Works created as ONA are still very rare, and usually their duration is short. An example of this format is the 4-minute add-on to the Azumanga Daioh series: Azumanga WEB Daioh

The transfer of emotions in anime is an occasion for a separate discussion. In addition to the traditional for anime types of manifestation of feelings by a character - a change in facial expression or tone of voice, a number of other techniques are used. Emotions can be depicted in an emphatically unrealistic, exaggerated way - the characters speak with their eyes closed to convey peremptoryness, or take on a picturesquely demonic appearance when they show anger.

In comedy situations, in order to show the frivolity of feelings, pictograms are used, such as a picture of “droplets of sweat” or “swollen veins” that appear on top of the hero’s head, or in a frame above it.
